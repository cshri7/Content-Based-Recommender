Keywords are special tokens in the language which have reserved use in the language. Keywords may not be used as identifiers in Java  you cannot declare a field whose name is a keyword, for instance. Examples of keywords are the primitive types, int and boolean; the control flow statements for and if; access modifiers such as public, and special words which mark the declaration and definition of Java classes, packages, and interfaces: class, package, interface.
